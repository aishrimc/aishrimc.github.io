Intro

Image adapted from: Mazumder et al. 2020

I am an observational cosmologist working with the largest structures in the Universe. My research interests are line intensity mapping, 21-cm cosmology, observational probes of the epoch of reionization and large scale structures.

I use observations from radio telescopes that can detect very long wavelengths that reach us after travesing for billions of lightyears in the cosmos.

I completed my Ph.D. from IIT Indore in India where I worked on using radio observations for observing the Universe during the first billion years of its formation. The first structures in the Universe formed and ionized the surrounding medium. This era is the epoch of reionization .
I am now using observations from radio interferometrs to analyse how this signal evolved over the last few billion years

Outside research, I contribute to Equity, Diversity and Inclusion efforts, mentoring and outreach activities.

Some Research Highlights

HI Intensity Mapping with the MIGHTEE Survey

In this project, I led the development of an analysis methodology for increasing signal-to-noise for detecting 21-cm power spectrum from large surveys. We showed that the method was feasible with observations from MIGHTEE survey, placing the first upper limits on the neutral hydrogen power spectrum at redshift below 0.5.

Entering HI Dominated Regime with Single Dish Radio Telescopes

In this work, we present the deepest single-dish HI intensity maps from the MeerKLASS survey spanning 2362 in the MeerKAT L-band. Cross-correlation with the galaxy positions from the Galaxy and Mass Assembly spectroscopic survey yielded a 4σ detection of the cross-power spectrum and present evidence of HI emission from stacking the maps onto the positions of the GAMA galaxies

ðŸ“„ Reference: MeerKLASS L-band deep-field intensity maps: entering the HI dominated regime

Simulating Sytematics

This work presents an end-to-end pipeline dealing with synthetic interferometric data of sensitive radio observations. The pipeline can incorporate astrophysical signals and systematic errors to quantify the error on the recovered signal and its effect on parameter inference.

ðŸ“„ Reference: Observing the Reionization : Effect of Calibration and Position Errors on Realistic Observation Conditions

Cosmology with Radio Continuum

In this project, we use radio-selected sources to map the distribution of matter in the Universe. Using observations from the Giant Metrewave Radio Telescope, we measure the spatial correlation length and the bias with respect to the underlying matter distribution, revealing interesting mismatch between observations and theoretical models.
